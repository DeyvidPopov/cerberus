# Mouse-dynamics detector comparison

Generated by `npm run eval:mouse --workspace @cerberus/server` (ADR-0014).
Reproducible: fixed seed `20240601`; re-running yields identical numbers.

- **Protocol:** per-user, mirroring the keystroke setup (Killourhy & Maxion style).
  Per user: train on the first 100 of the user's genuine
  feature windows; test on the held-out genuine windows + the first
  30 windows of every OTHER user (impostors).
- **Users:** 10 · **Feature dimension:** 9
  (velocity / acceleration / curvature / click + pause statistics — the deployed
  M10 `extractMouseWindowFeatures`, over a 32-sample
  window; non-overlapping for the benchmark).
- **Dataset:** Balabit Mouse Dynamics Challenge (`docs\evaluation\data\balabit`),
  NOT committed — real human captures, PROJECT.md §5. At the equal-error operating
  point FAR = FRR = EER.

| Detector | mean EER | SD EER | mean FAR | mean FRR |
|----------|---------:|-------:|---------:|---------:|
| mahalanobis | 38.18% | 7.82% | 38.18% | 38.18% |
| one-class-svm | 35.94% | 2.86% | 35.94% | 35.94% |
| isolation-forest | 34.95% | 6.50% | 34.95% | 34.95% |

_Mouse dynamics is a noisier modality than keystroke: per-user EER is higher and the
SD across users is larger (movement style varies more by task/hardware than fixed-text
typing). The SD column is reported precisely because the per-user spread matters — a
mean alone would understate the variability. These numbers describe the per-window
discriminability of the deployed extractor; the live system smooths windows with an
EWMA before acting (ADR-0013), trading latency for far fewer false locks._
